We must unite against COVID-19

Follow these 8 precautions to strike-out the spread of
CORONAVIRUS

Wash Your Hands more Frequently

Note just wash but wash your hands thoroughly with an alcohol-based hand rub or wash them with soap and water. It kills the viruses that may be on your hands.

Don't go that close to the one who is coughing or sneezing

Try maintaining at least 3-foot distance between yourself and anyone who is coughing or sneezing. If you are too close, you can breathe in the droplets, including the COVID-19 virus if the person coughing has the disease.

Shun touching eyes, nose and mouth

Your hands touch several surface and can pick up viruses. Once contaminated, hands can transfer the virus to your eyes, nose or mouth. The virus can enter your body from there and can make you sick.

When and where to use a mask?

Wear a mask if you are taking care of a person with suspected Coronavirus infection
Wear a mask if you are coughing and sneezing

Be a Hero! Take responsibility to protect others

Avoid spitting in public
Avoid close contact when experiencing cough and fever
If suffering from fever, cough and difficulty breathing seek medical care immediately

You need to follow respiratory hygiene

You must cover your mouth and nose with your bent elbow or tissue when you cough and sneeze. Dispose of the used tissue immediately.

Cope up with stress and mental condition!

It's normal to be stressed, talk to your friends and family! That helps indeed! Maintain a healthy lifestyle- including proper diet, sleep and exercise. Don't use smoking, alcohol or other drugs to deal with emotions

Stay Informed via internet and newspaper

Stay informed on the latest developments about Coronavirus. Follow the advice given by your healthcare provider and local public health authority or your employer on how to protect yourself and others.

Most Frequently asked questions about
CORONAVIRUS

If Traveling on a plane, How do I stay safe?

It is recommended to avoid traveling to countries that are highly infacted. Stay safe by using hand sanitizer that contains 60%-90% alcohol or antiseptic hand wipes, which you can also use to wipe down arm rests, remote controls at your seat and your tray table.

01

What is the incubation period for COVID-19?

The "incubation period" means the time between catching the virus and beginning to have symptoms of the disease. According to WHO, the incubation period for COVID-19 ranges from 1-14 days, most commanly around five days.

02

Can humans become infected with COVID-19 from an animal source?

Possible animal source of COVID-19 have not yet been confirmed, So protect yourself from visiting animal markets and direct contacts with animal. Practice good food safely, avoid consuming raw or undercooked animal products.

03

What does self-isolation involve?

Self-isolation involve staying indoors like not going to work, school or other public places, and avoiding contact with other people for 14 days. You also stay away from people in your home. Stay away from the pets - if unavoidable, wash your hands before and after contact.

04

Can the COVID-19 virus spread through drinking water?

The COVID-19 virus has not been detected in drinking water. Conventional water treatment methods that use filtration and disinfection, such as those in most municipal drinking water system, should remove or inactivate the virus that causes COVID-19.

05

Are there any medicines that cure COVID-19?

There is no evidence that current medicine can prevent or cure the disease. While some western, traditional or home remedies may provide comfort and alleviate symptoms of COVID-19 but WHO does not recommend self-medicaion and several ongoing clinical trials.

06

Is it safe for mother to breastfeed if she is infected with COVID-19?

No evidence of the virus has been found in the breast milk of women with COVID-19. ECDC suggests that all pregnant women follow the same precautions for the prevention of COVID-19, including regular handwashing and self-isolating in case of any symptoms.

07

Should I wear a medical mask to protect Myself?

Wear a mask if you are showing COVID-19 symptoms or in case contact with someone infected with COVID-19. WHO urges people to use masks wisely and Indian Health Ministry officials also suggest when a person maintains effective social distace, a mask is not needed.

08

How long can the COVID-19 last on surfaces?

There is no certainly about how long the virus that causes COVID-19 survives on surface, but it behaves like other coronaviruses. WHO suggests that coronavirus may remain on suface for a few hour or up to several days and this may vary under different conditions.

RUMOUR VS FACT COVID-19
STAY SAFE, STAY INFORMED

RUMOUR

Wearing a face mask prevents from getting infected by COVID-19

FACT

Wear a mask if you are showing COVID-19 symptoms or in case contact with someone infected with COVID-19. WHO urges people to use masks wisely and Indian Health Ministry officials also suggest when a person maintains effective social distace, a mask is not needed.

RUMOUR

Gargling with luke-warm water and salt prevents COVID-19

FACT

In an email to AFP,the WHO said,'We have seen no convincing evidence that rinsing one's mouth with diluted saline water will provide protection from this disease'. In a press release, UNICEF has also clarified that the message circulating on social media is not true.

RUMOUR

COVID-19 can survive up to 12 hrs on metal, 9 hrs on clothes, and 10 minutes on the human hands.

FACT

According to WHO, there is no evidence if Coronavirus may remain on surfaces for a few hours or upto a few days, this may vary under different conditions like type of surface, temperature or humidity of the environment.


RUMOUR

Hot temperature will stop the COVID-19 outbreak

FACT

"It is not known whether the spread of COVID-19 will decrease when the weather becomes warmer. There is much more to learn about the transmissibility,Severity, and other feature associated with COVID-19 and investigations are ongoing", according to the CDC and WHO.

RUMOUR

COVID-19 is nothing worse than winter flu

FACT

Considering the current estimates of a roughly 1% fatality rate of COVID-19, it is about 10 times deadlier than seasonal flu, says Dr. Bruce Aylward, head of a WHO-China joint mission and veteran epidemiologist.

RUMOUR

Garlic intake can help in curing COVID-19

FACT

In response to this rumor, WHO says it is a "healthy food that may have some antimicrobial properties", but there's no evidence that eating garlic can protect people from the new coronavirus.
